## Executive Summary

We analyzed Parkside Advisory Group's public site on April 27, 2026, including 7 core pages, robots.txt, sitemap.xml, llms.txt, live metadata, structured data, crawler access, and a competitor benchmark against Gadoci Consulting. Parkside earns a GEO Readiness Score of **67/100**, which places the brand in the **Moderate** tier: the technical foundation is strong enough for AI discovery, but the site needs deeper authority and citation-ready content to compete in AI-generated answers. The single most important finding is that Parkside is accessible and AI-friendly at the technical level, but Gadoci currently has a much stronger public content footprint and author-entity presence. The top priorities are to verify canonical DNS stability, add route-specific metadata and schema, and publish practical answer-focused resources around workflow automation, intake, follow-up, and AI guardrails. Addressing these recommendations could improve AI-driven discovery by an estimated **20-35%** over time; without analytics access, the conservative benchmark value is approximately **$750-$2,500 per month in additional organic media value**, not a guaranteed revenue outcome.

## Citability Analysis

### Top 5 Most Citable Pages

1. **https://parksideag.com/**
   - Why it is citable: Clear positioning, 736 words, strong H1, practical workflow problems, human review guardrails, and a concrete process.
   - Improvement: Add a direct answer block titled "What does Parkside Advisory Group do?" near the top of the page.

2. **https://parksideag.com/blueprint**
   - Why it is citable: Explains the primary offer and frames the Blueprint around one real workflow.
   - Improvement: Add "What the Blueprint includes," "What Parkside needs from your team," and "What happens after the audit" sections.

3. **https://parksideag.com/services**
   - Why it is citable: Lists service lines that map to common AI search queries: Blueprint, workflow automation, AI agent builds, and operating support.
   - Improvement: Add best-fit, inputs, outputs, timeline range, and not-a-fit guidance for each service.

4. **https://parksideag.com/use-cases**
   - Why it is citable: Targets practical operational use cases where AI answers can cite examples.
   - Improvement: Expand each use case into a short pattern: trigger, owner, automation role, human review point, and success signal.

5. **https://parksideag.com/llms.txt**
   - Why it is citable: Gives AI systems a direct summary of Parkside's core pages, services, and guardrails.
   - Improvement: Add more citation-ready facts, preferred descriptions, and page-by-page summaries.

### Top 5 Least Citable Pages

1. **https://parksideag.com/about**
   - Why it is unlikely to be cited: The page is only 178 words and lacks named expertise, public profile links, credentials, or detailed methodology.
   - Recommendation: Add leadership, operating experience, methodology, service area, and review principles.

2. **https://parksideag.com/privacy**
   - Why it is unlikely to be cited: It is intentionally legal/administrative and not a discovery page.
   - Recommendation: Keep concise, but add last-updated date and Organization schema linkage.

3. **https://parksideag.com/intake**
   - Why it is unlikely to be cited: It is a conversion page, not an educational page.
   - Recommendation: Keep focused on form completion; add a short "what happens next" section for clarity.

4. **https://parksideag.com/use-cases**
   - Why it is under-citable despite a good topic: It is only 295 words and lacks detailed examples.
   - Recommendation: Add 5-7 specific workflow patterns with direct answers.

5. **https://parksideag.com/services**
   - Why it is under-citable despite commercial importance: It summarizes offers but lacks enough detail for AI systems to quote.
   - Recommendation: Add service-level detail and matching Service schema.

Your most citable pages are your best candidates for appearing in AI-generated answers. Improving the least citable pages is the highest-return content investment because those pages already target high-intent service and trust questions.

### Where Parkside Leads

Parkside leads on AI-specific crawler guidance because `/llms.txt` is live and the site has a clean Organization/ProfessionalService/OfferCatalog schema baseline. Parkside also has sharper service positioning around missed follow-up, workflow handoffs, and operating systems, which is easier to turn into high-intent AI search content.

### Where Parkside Trails

Gadoci leads on content footprint, author attribution, canonical metadata, and public search presence. Its sitemap exposes a large article library, and sampled article pages include Article, Person, Organization, WebPage, dates, topics, and author signals. This gives AI systems more material to cite and more reasons to trust the brand as a topical authority.

### Competitive Recommendation

Parkside should not try to copy Gadoci's volume. Parkside should win on specificity. A focused library of practical resources about intake automation, missed follow-up, service workflows, reporting visibility, human review, and automation guardrails will create a stronger niche signal than broad AI transformation commentary.

### Recommended Content Map

The competitor benchmark shows that AI search visibility improves when a site has enough public pages to answer adjacent questions, not only the main commercial question. Parkside's best opportunity is to build a practical library around operating problems that buyers already recognize. Each resource should answer one specific question, include a concise definition near the top, explain when the issue matters, list the inputs required to solve it, describe the human review point, and link back to the most relevant service page.

Recommended first resources:

| Resource | Primary Question Answered | Target Page Link |
|---|---|---|
| Missed Follow-Up Workflow Guide | How do businesses prevent leads and estimates from going cold? | `/services` |
| Intake Automation Checklist | What information should be captured before automating intake? | `/blueprint` |
| AI Workflow Guardrails | What should not be automated without human review? | `/use-cases` |
| Handoff Visibility Playbook | How do teams make work ownership visible across tools? | `/services` |
| Reporting Automation Primer | Which recurring reports are good candidates for automation? | `/use-cases` |
| Blueprint Methodology Page | What happens during an AI Operations Blueprint? | `/blueprint` |

This content should be written as practical operating guidance, not broad AI commentary. That gives Parkside a clearer angle than Gadoci's broader AI transformation library and makes each page easier for AI systems to cite in answer boxes.

### Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| GEO | Generative Engine Optimization - optimizing content to be cited by AI search platforms |
| AIO | AI Overviews - Google's AI-generated answer boxes at the top of search results |
| E-E-A-T | Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness |
| SSR | Server-Side Rendering - generating HTML on the server so crawlers can read content without JavaScript |
| CWV | Core Web Vitals - Google's page experience metrics |
| LCP | Largest Contentful Paint - time to render the largest visible element |
| INP | Interaction to Next Paint - responsiveness metric |
| CLS | Cumulative Layout Shift - visual stability metric |
| JSON-LD | JavaScript Object Notation for Linked Data - preferred structured data format |
| sameAs | Schema.org property linking an entity to its official profiles on other platforms |
| IndexNow | Protocol for notifying search engines of content changes |
| llms.txt | Proposed file standard for guiding AI systems about a site's content |
| SERP | Search Engine Results Page |
| Topical Authority | The depth and breadth of a site's coverage of its core subject |
